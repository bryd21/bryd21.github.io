Founder & Head Coach

Steven Park

A lifelong passion for tennis, transformed into a mission to inspire and develop the next generation of players.

Steven Park's journey in tennis began at age seven when he picked up his first racket at a local community center. What started as a casual hobby quickly evolved into a burning passion that would define his life's work. Throughout his junior career, Steven competed in tournaments across the Northeast, developing not just his technical skills but also a deep understanding of the mental and strategic aspects of the game.

His dedication earned him a Division I tennis scholarship, where he honed his skills while studying Sports Science and Psychology. This unique combination of on-court experience and academic knowledge would later become the foundation of his coaching philosophy. After graduating, Steven spent two years training with top coaches in Europe, learning diverse coaching methodologies and gaining exposure to different playing styles.

Upon returning to the United States, Steven founded Modu Tennis with a clear vision: to create a training program that goes beyond just hitting balls. His approach integrates technical excellence, mental fortitude, physical conditioning, and character development. "Tennis is more than a sport," Steven often says. "It's a vehicle for teaching life lessons—discipline, resilience, sportsmanship, and the pursuit of continuous improvement."

Over the past 15 years, Steven has worked with hundreds of students, from complete beginners taking their first steps on the court to advanced players competing at national tournaments. His students have gone on to play collegiate tennis, with several earning scholarships to prestigious universities. But for Steven, success isn't measured solely by trophies and rankings—it's about instilling a genuine love for the game and watching his students grow both as players and individuals.

Steven holds certifications from both the USPTA (United States Professional Tennis Association) and PTR (Professional Tennis Registry), and regularly attends coaching seminars to stay current with the latest developments in tennis instruction and sports science. When he's not on the court teaching, you'll find him competing in regional tournaments or analyzing professional matches to identify new techniques and strategies to share with his students.

Core Values

Our Coaching Philosophy

Four pillars that guide everything we do at Modu Tennis

01

Technical Excellence

We believe that mastery begins with fundamentals. Every stroke, every movement, every tactical decision is built on a foundation of proper technique. Our progressive curriculum ensures that students develop correct form from day one, preventing bad habits and accelerating long-term improvement.

02

Mental Strength

Tennis is often called the most mental sport, and we take that seriously. We teach players how to stay composed under pressure, maintain focus during long matches, and develop the mental resilience needed to overcome setbacks. Confidence is built through preparation and positive reinforcement.

03

Individualized Approach

No two players are exactly alike. We recognize that each student has unique strengths, learning styles, and goals. Our coaching adapts to the individual, creating personalized training plans that maximize each player's potential while respecting their pace and preferred learning methods.

04

Lifelong Love

While we prepare competitive players for tournaments, our ultimate goal is to foster a genuine, lasting love for tennis. We want our students to enjoy the game for decades to come, whether they're competing at the collegiate level or playing socially with friends on weekends.
